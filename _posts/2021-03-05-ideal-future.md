For me, an ideal medium-term future would have the following characteristics:

	- A cosmopolitan, decentralized society where:
	
		○ People are educated to their hearts' content for free
		
		○ People enjoy lots of free time for leisure
		
		○ Social decisions are made smoothly and democratically, with input from both citizens and experts
		
		○ Science is standardized but decentralized, so that new ideas or information can easily challenge the status quo, and there is a central running list of research questions for people to answer
		
		○ The market is either fully driven by AI, or driven enough so that essential goods and services are post-scarce
		
		○ Animals are no longer killed for meat, and lab-grown meat takes over
		
		○ Food is grown sustainably and locally, and food waste is used as compost
		
		○ The world runs on renewable energy, and environmental destruction is mitigated
		
		○ Products are rarely wasted, and the circular economy takes over
		
			§ Most products are either 3D printed at home, or produced/delivered in an automatic way with reusable packaging
			
		○ People live kindly next to each other, and mechanisms are in place to combat people being left behind
		
		○ We have a comprehensive database of all threats that humans face, and how we can carefully avoid them (and this is reflected in policy)
		
		○ Cooperation and public benefit investment is the predominant mode of human organization
		
		○ Social status is less important
		
		○ The education system is personalized, and people are given lessons that build their capacity for critical thinking
		
		○ Humans have the capacity to manage the weather
		
		○ We have automated processes for medicine, medical research, mental health treatment
		
		○ New insights and data are included in decision-making
		
		○ Inequality does not matter as much, because of the prevalence of global public goods
		
		○ A check system is developed to prevent conspiracy theories, polarization, or government capture
		
		○ Medicine has progressed to fully understand the human body, and is readily available to everyone
		
		○ Everyone has access to the basic standards of living
		
		○ Mental issues are few and far between

Added benefits would include the following:

	- Knowledge or consensus on deep philosophical matters
	
	- A holistic understanding of physics
	
	- Goods are delivered efficiently, or 3D printed at home based on scanners
	
	- Wild animals are no longer suffering

How far away are we from this?

My estimate: 100 years, if we don't destroy ourselves before then.

The state of the world is presently very bad, but we are moving very quickly to a much better place, thanks to the benevolence of human nature.
